## More ways to use favorites

AI meals now appear in your history and can be saved as favorites. This makes a meal from Intake AI just as easy to find and use again as any other entry.

![sharing](assets/ai-favorites-en.mp4)

Larger favorite collections are easier to manage too. You can now sort favorites alphabetically, by date, or by log count, so frequently used foods and recently saved favorites are only a few taps away.

![sharing](assets/favorites-sorting-en.mp4)

## More precise portions

On iOS and Android, you can now enter decimal gram amounts. Small portions and exact quantities can be tracked just as precisely as you weighed them.

## A better manual workout workflow

![sharing](assets/activity-sheet-en.mp4)

The manual workout screen on iOS and Android has been rebuilt as a complete form. Existing workouts are shown in a list, making them easier to review at a glance.

When adding a workout, you can let Intake calculate the calories burned or enter calories manually. This gives you the choice between a quick estimate and using a value you already know.

## More reliable day to day

On iOS, you can now manually sync workouts from previous days. Values changed while editing a recipe are saved reliably without requiring you to leave the field first. Workouts also no longer appear unnamed with a duration of 0 minutes.

On Android, the barcode scanner now recognizes barcodes correctly on the Samsung Galaxy A23.

You can find the full changelog [here](https://featurevoting.tobibechtold.dev/app/intake/changelog).
