# German Copy Authenticity Design

## Goal

Make the German landing page copy feel native, direct, and everyday-authentic instead of translated. This work applies only to the new conversion-focused sections added in the redesign.

## Fixed Constraints

- do not change the FAQ section
- do not change the Product Updates section
- keep the existing structure and UX of the page
- focus on wording, not feature scope

## Scope

Update only the German copy in these areas:

- hero
- why switch
- comparison
- proof stories
- reviews summary
- roadmap / feature voting
- final CTA

## Problem

The current German copy is understandable, but several lines feel like direct translations from strong English product marketing. The friction is mostly in tone:

- phrasing is too literal in places
- some wording feels over-polished or abstract
- a few lines read like campaign copy rather than normal German product copy

This weakens trust because the page is trying to sell a simple, honest product.

## Approved Tone Direction

Recommended direction:

- direct
- everyday-native
- clear
- slightly blunt where the USP matters
- not aggressive
- not overly polished

The German should sound like a native app landing page, not a localization layer.

## Writing Principles

### Keep It Concrete

Prefer concrete value statements over abstract framing.

Prefer:

- `ohne Abo`
- `ohne Konto`
- `deine Daten bleiben auf deinem Gerät`

Avoid wording that feels inflated or vague, such as:

- `ernsthaftes Ernährungstracking`
- `ruhiger Kalorientracker`
- `Datenschutz-Kompromisse`

### Use Shorter Sentences

The English source can support longer product-marketing sentences. The German version should be tighter and more conversational.

### Sound Like Real German Product Copy

Use phrasing that feels natural on a German app page:

- `Kein Konto nötig`
- `Kein Bock mehr auf Abos`
- `Wenn du nur dein Essen tracken willst ...`

Avoid lines that sound translated or overly formal.

### Keep The Strongest USP Extremely Short

Where the page states the core promise, the copy should be very compact:

- `Kein Abo. Kein Konto.`
- `Deine Daten bleiben auf deinem Gerät.`

## Section Direction

### Hero

The hero should stop sounding like a translated campaign headline and instead feel immediately understandable.

Direction:

- replace `Wechsle zu Kalorientracking` with a more natural action-oriented line
- keep `ohne Abo` as the strongest short hook
- rewrite the body to be simpler and less formal
- simplify the secondary CTA

### Why Switch

This section should sound relatable and slightly more everyday-native than the rest of the page.

Direction:

- rewrite the intro paragraph in plainspoken German
- make the subscription pain more direct
- remove translated-feeling phrases like `Zwangskonto`
- keep privacy language simple and factual

### Comparison

The table copy should stay short and highly scannable.

Direction:

- keep labels simple
- use category language Germans would read quickly
- avoid over-explaining in the subtitle

### Proof Stories

This section currently feels the most translated.

Direction:

- replace slogan-like lines with practical language
- focus on what feels better in day-to-day use
- avoid abstract framing like `Beweise statt Versprechen`

### Reviews Summary

The summary should sound credible and restrained.

Direction:

- avoid lofty formulations
- describe the audience in simpler German

### Roadmap / Feature Voting

This section should feel more human and less like a translated community CTA.

Direction:

- use simple collaborative phrasing
- keep the call to action short

### Final CTA

The final CTA should restate the value proposition in the most natural, compact German possible.

Direction:

- focus on `kein Abo`, `kein Login-Zwang`, `deine Daten bleiben auf deinem Gerät`
- avoid metaphorical wording like `ruhiger`

## Example Direction

Examples of the approved tone:

- `Kalorien tracken, ohne Abo.`
- `Warum viele zu Intake wechseln`
- `Kein Konto nötig`
- `Warum Intake im Alltag besser funktioniert`
- `Hilf mit, Intake weiter besser zu machen.`
- `Wechsle zu einem Kalorientracker ohne Abo.`

## Success Criteria

The rewrite is successful if:

- the new German sections read like they were written in German first
- the copy feels simpler and more trustworthy
- the wording supports the product USP without sounding salesy
- FAQ and Product Updates remain unchanged
