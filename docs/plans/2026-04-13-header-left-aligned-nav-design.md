# Header Left-Aligned Nav Design

**Context**

The header currently distributes three visual zones across the full width: logo on the left, nav in the middle, and controls on the right. With only a small number of nav items, the centered nav makes the bar feel tighter and more fragmented than necessary.

**Decision**

- Group the logo and top navigation into a single left-side cluster.
- Keep the language switcher and download CTA on the right.
- Preserve the current plain-text link styling and spacing treatment.
- Do not introduce dropdowns or additional header levels.

**Rationale**

This should make the header feel calmer and easier to scan. The main brand/navigation block reads as one intentional unit, while the utility actions remain clearly separated on the right. It improves hierarchy without changing the information architecture.
